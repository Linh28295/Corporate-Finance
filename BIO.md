# Bio — Nguyen Bui Ngoc Linh

Nguyen Bui Ngoc Linh is a Senior Manager of Operations & Technology Strategy and Transformation at Prudential Vietnam, where she leads enterprise-wide change across a multi-million dollar transformation portfolio. With eight years of progressive experience across life insurance, retail, and financial services in Vietnam, her career has been grounded in corporate strategy — she has held dedicated strategy roles at three organizations before moving into enterprise transformation leadership.

Her strategic foundation was built progressively: as Assistant to the Chief Strategy Officer at PNJ Group, she developed core competencies in business planning and competitive analysis; as Business Strategy Supervisor at AIA Vietnam, she advanced performance improvement and strategic reporting capabilities; and as Manager of Corporate Strategy at Prudential Vietnam, she led enterprise-wide strategic planning, translated executive priorities into measurable execution plans, and drove cross-functional alignment across business units. In her current role, she applies that strategic depth to leading Target Operating Model (TOM) design, operating model refinement, and large-scale transformation governance.

Key achievements include delivering **20% cost optimization** through process re-engineering and operating model redesign, and receiving Prudential's **Alexandrite Award** for pivotal contributions to the Health Strategy and Claims Business Process Re-engineering initiative. Her expertise spans corporate strategy, portfolio governance, change management, TOM design, and financial stewardship.

Linh holds a Bachelor of Commerce in Marketing and Management from Curtin Singapore and is pursuing an Executive MBA at the University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa's Shidler College of Business, with a focus on corporate finance, valuation, and enterprise leadership. She is PROSCI-certified in change management and proficient in Celonis process mining, advanced financial modeling, and data-driven performance frameworks.

Her long-term goal is to shape the future of financial services in Southeast Asia by integrating technology-driven strategy with sustainable, people-centered operating models.
